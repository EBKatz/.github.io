Bio: Ethan B. Katz is a historian and author who seeks to illuminate the crises of today by analyzing imprints of the past. He is Associate Professor of History and Jewish Studies and Faculty Director of the Center for Jewish Studies at UC Berkeley. He is the author of The Burdens of Brotherhood: Jews and Muslims from North Africa to France (Harvard, 2015), winner of the National Jewish Book Award and four other major prizes.

Speaking: Available for lectures, panels, and media commentary on antisemitism, Jewish-Muslim relations, French history, and campus climate issues. He has spoken at universities throughout the U.S., Europe, and Israel.
