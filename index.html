Ethan B. Katz

Historian · UC Berkeley

Ethan B. Katz is a historian and author who illuminates the crises of today by analyzing imprints of the past. He is Associate Professor of History and Jewish Studies and Faculty Director of the Center for Jewish Studies at UC Berkeley. His scholarship focuses on modern Jewish history, Jewish-Muslim relations, and questions of belonging and exclusion in Europe and the Middle East.

Featured Work

The Burdens of Brotherhood

Harvard University Press, 2015

Jews and Muslims from North Africa to France — tracing their encounters from World War I to the 2015 Paris attacks, showing how these relations were shaped by everyday encounters and their triangular relationship with the French state.

🏆 National Jewish Book Award · J. Russell Major Prize · American Library in Paris Book Award · David H. Pinkney Prize

Ethan also co-founded Berkeley's Antisemitism Education Initiative and chairs the Chancellor's Advisory Committee on Jewish Student Life. He is a frequent analyst and speaker on antisemitism, Islamophobia, and democratic culture in the U.S., France, and elsewhere.
