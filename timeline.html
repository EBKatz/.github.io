A Century of Jewish-Muslim Relations in France

Prof. Katz's research traces Jewish-Muslim encounters from the Dreyfus Affair to today, revealing a history far more complex than headlines suggest.

1870

Crémieux Decree

France grants citizenship to Algerian Jews but not Muslims, creating a legal divide that would shape colonial society for nearly a century.

1894

The Dreyfus Affair Begins

Captain Alfred Dreyfus, a Jewish French army officer, is falsely convicted of treason. The affair splits France and crystallizes modern antisemitism as a political force.

1914

World War I Begins

Jews and Muslims from North Africa fight together in French trenches — the first large-scale encounter between these communities on French soil.

1926

Grande Mosquée de Paris Opens

France builds its first major mosque, partly to honor Muslim soldiers. The mosque would later play a contested role during WWII.

1934

Constantine Riots

Deadly violence between Muslims and Jews in Algeria kills 25 Jews and 3 Muslims — a rupture whose causes have been meticulously uncovered by historians.

1940–43

The Algiers Underground

Mostly Jewish resistance fighters work alongside French officers — some of them antisemites — to help secure Operation Torch. The subject of Prof. Katz's forthcoming book.

1954–62

Algerian War of Independence

A brutal eight-year conflict reshapes North Africa. Jews find themselves caught between French colonial rule and Algerian nationalism.

1962

Algerian Independence

Mass migration brings hundreds of thousands of Jews and Muslims from North Africa to mainland France, transforming both communities.

1967

Six-Day War

Israel's war with its Arab neighbors reverberates in France, straining Jewish-Muslim relations and reshaping diaspora politics.

2000

Second Intifada Begins

Violence in Israel-Palestine sparks a wave of antisemitic incidents in France, raising alarm about imported conflict.

2015

Charlie Hebdo & Hyper Cacher

Terrorist attacks at a satirical magazine and a kosher supermarket raise urgent questions about coexistence — questions with deep historical roots.

Today

The Present Crisis

Antisemitism and Islamophobia persist. Understanding how these hatreds have long been intertwined is essential to addressing them.
