Freeing the Empire

Forthcoming

The Uprising of Jews and Antisemites That Helped Win World War II. This book tells the untold story of the mostly Jewish resistance fighters who helped secure Operation Torch in Algiers — working alongside French officers, some of whom were antisemites.

The Burdens of Brotherhood

Harvard University Press, 2015

Jews and Muslims from North Africa to France. Headlines from France suggest that Muslims have renewed an age-old struggle against Jews. But the past tells a different story. Focusing on the experiences of ordinary people, this book traces Jewish-Muslim encounters from World War I to the 2015 Paris attacks.

🏆 National Jewish Book Award · J. Russell Major Prize (AHA) · American Library in Paris Book Award · David H. Pinkney Prize (SFHS)

Colonialism and the Jews

Indiana University Press, 2017 · Co-edited with Lisa Moses Leff and Maud Mandel

The most extended examination to date of Jewish history in light of the new colonial history. Includes a section on "Zionism and Colonialism" widely praised as among the most rigorous and balanced treatments of that complex topic.

🏆 Finalist, National Jewish Book Award

Secularism in Question

University of Pennsylvania Press, 2015 · Co-edited with Ari Joskowicz

Jews and Judaism in Modern Times. This volume illustrates how the categories of "religious" and "secular" have frequently proven far more permeable than fixed, challenging assumptions about the development of secularism that emerge from Protestant European and American perspectives.

Routledge, 2023 · Co-edited with Elisha Ancselovits and Sergey Dolgopolski

Between the University and the Beit Midrash. An interdisciplinary volume exploring how traditions of Jewish argument and interpretation intersect with academic inquiry.
