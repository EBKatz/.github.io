Berkeley Bridging Fellowship Program

Fall 2025 & Spring 2026 · 2 credits per semester · Co-convened with Prof. Asad Ahmed

A unique forum and community-building space focused on cultivating meaningful, supported conversations about Israel and Palestine. The fellowship brings together a diverse cohort of undergraduate students for dialogue sessions led by experienced facilitators, content sessions with leading experts, and skill-building retreats. Fellows develop tangible skills for discussing sensitive topics, build relationships across communities, and design capstone initiatives to extend their learning.

The program meets Tuesdays 3–5 PM for content sessions, plus several Sunday retreats throughout each semester. It culminates in a meeting with the UC Berkeley Chancellor and student-designed capstone projects.

Courses Taught

At UC Berkeley, Ethan teaches courses on Jewish history, Muslim-Jewish encounters, Zionism and Israel, modern France and its empire, the Middle East and North Africa, and historical methodologies. He also leads workshops on historical and contemporary antisemitism for staff, faculty, students, and community members.
